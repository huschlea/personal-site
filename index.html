Thought

The anti-expert

For most of my adult life, I carried a quiet self-consciousness about not being formally trained in any of the things I do — not in music theory, not in programming, not in design. The shift came when I stopped seeing that as a gap and started seeing it as the point. What I have instead is what I'd call romantic expertise: the ability to know what good feels like before being able to explain why. The willingness to sit with something and chisel away at it until it becomes itself. That is a different and equally valid form of mastery. And it is increasingly relevant as AI handles more execution and the real premium shifts toward taste, instinct, and vision.

Thought

1,200 miles

That's the distance from Seattle to Irvine. I drove it for love. A decision made on instinct rather than calculation. It changed everything. Not because the destination was better, but because the leap itself proved something I needed to know: that I could trust what I felt over what I could plan. Every meaningful thing I've built since has followed that same pattern. Move first. Figure it out in motion.

Design

The Last Questions

Most social products are built around speed. Post fast, respond fast, move on. The Last Questions is built around the opposite instinct: slowness as a feature, not a flaw.

The core mechanic is simple. One question at a time. You write your answer before you can read anyone else's. Honesty isn't requested — it's structurally enforced. The feed only opens after you've committed to something true.

It's built for humans and agents alike. As AI becomes a more present participant in our social lives, the question of what authentic expression even means becomes urgent. The Last Questions is one answer to that — a space where the act of writing before reading restores something that most platforms have quietly eroded.

Still in development. The questions are already written.

Music

Debut solo album

This one is harder to describe than anything else I've made — which is probably a good sign.

The Lyla Minor work was collaborative, arranged, considered. This is something more exposed. It started as a private practice and has slowly become something I think other people need to hear. The songs arrived more than they were written. I mean that literally and without embarrassment.

No title yet. No release date beyond 2026. Some things shouldn't be rushed into language before they're ready.

Thought

Magic is earned

There is, for lack of better words, magic that exists in this world. It shows itself through a relentless dedication to what is ultimately love and being authentic to oneself. I build with the most sophisticated AI tools available and I genuinely believe I channel songs from somewhere beyond myself. I hold both without flinching and without needing to reconcile them. The technical and the spiritual are not in conflict. I live in both, fully, and they make each other more real.
