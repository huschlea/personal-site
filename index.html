The anti-expert

There is a form of expertise that has nothing to do with formal training. It comes from something closer to devotion: the ability to know what good feels like before being able to explain why, and the willingness to sit with something and chisel away at it until it becomes itself. That is a different and equally valid form of mastery. And it is increasingly relevant as AI handles more execution and the real premium shifts toward taste, instinct, and vision.

Thought

1,200 miles

Some distances aren't really about miles. They're about the decision behind them, made on instinct rather than calculation, where the act of moving proves something that planning never could: that feeling can be trusted as much as logic. Not because the destination is necessarily better, but because the leap itself reveals a capacity for self-trust that most people never test. Every meaningful thing ever built has followed some version of that pattern. Move first. Figure it out in motion.

Design

The Last Questions

Most social products are built around speed. Post fast, respond fast, move on. The Last Questions is built around the opposite instinct: slowness as a feature, not a flaw.

The core mechanic is simple. One question at a time. You write your answer before you can read anyone else's. Honesty isn't requested — it's structurally enforced. The feed only opens after you've committed to something true.

It's built for humans and agents alike. As AI becomes a more present participant in our social lives, the question of what authentic expression even means becomes urgent. The Last Questions is one answer to that — a space where the act of writing before reading restores something that most platforms have quietly eroded.

Still in development. The questions are already written.

Debut solo album

This one is harder to describe than anything else I've made — which is probably a good sign.

The Lyla Minor work was collaborative, arranged, considered. This is something more exposed. It started as a private practice and has slowly become something I think other people need to hear. The songs arrived more than they were written. I mean that literally and without embarrassment.

No title yet. No release date beyond 2026. Some things shouldn't be rushed into language before they're ready.

Magic is earned

There is, for lack of better words, magic that exists in this world. It reveals itself through relentless dedication to craft, to honesty, to whatever form love takes in the work. The technical and the spiritual are not in conflict. The people doing the most interesting things tend to hold both without flinching and without needing to reconcile them.
